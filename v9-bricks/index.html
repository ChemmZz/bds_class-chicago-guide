01 — Concept

The exposed brick bar aesthetic

Chicago's Wicker Park, Logan Square, and Pilsen neighborhoods are full of spaces that converted industrial buildings into bars, studios, and coffee shops — keeping the exposed brick walls intact. That particular texture — rough red clay, mortar seams, worn wood — is as much Chicago as Mies van der Rohe.

"The best bars in Chicago look like they've been there since before the building had a name."

The CSS brick pattern on structural elements (the header) uses only repeating linear gradients — simulating the vertical mortar joints and horizontal course lines of actual brick masonry without any image assets.

02 — Palette

Brick red, mortar gray, deep brown, warm off-white

The palette is pulled directly from the materials: brick clay (#8b3a2a), mortar (#c8bfb0), the dark brown of old timber framing (#2d1a0e), and the warm off-white of aged plaster (#f2ede6). No pure whites or pure blacks — everything is slightly tinted with the warmth of age.

03 — Map

Warm desaturated tiles — the urban warmth

A light filter pulls the OSM tiles toward the warm desaturated tones of the palette — slightly sepia, slightly dim — without making the map hard to read. The brick red route polyline reads clearly against this treated background.

.leaflet-tile-pane {
  filter: saturate(0.6) brightness(0.95) sepia(0.15);
}
