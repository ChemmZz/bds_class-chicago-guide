Chicago Field Guide — Iteration 14

Autumn

Lincoln Square in October · Amber · Rust · Playfair 900 + Crimson Pro 300

Concept

Late October in Lincoln Square. The maples along Western Avenue have gone amber and rust. The German bakeries are pulling strudel. The farmers market is down to the last squash and the first cider. This is Chicago's kindest season.

The typographic experiment here is maximum weight contrast within a single serif category: Playfair Display at 900 weight for display text, Crimson Pro at 300 for body. The difference is nearly four stops of weight — dramatically legible.

Design Decisions

Playfair 900 + Crimson Pro 300 Nearly four stops of weight contrast between headline and body. Playfair's extreme thick-thin stroke contrast at 900 weight reads like carved wood type from a 19th century broadside.
Amber gradient header linear-gradient from amber to a warm harvest tan — the color of late afternoon sun through turning leaves.
Autumnal map filter sepia(0.35) saturate(1.2) brightness(0.96) hue-rotate(-8deg) warms the map tiles toward amber-gold, making the city look like it's photographed in October light.
Warm displacement shadows 2px 2px 0 rgba(44,26,8,0.12) — walnut-tinted shadows on cards, matching the deep brown text color.
