01 — Concept

Warm, tropical, joyful — without being kitsch

Hawaiian is the lightest and most joyful iteration. The challenge was capturing genuine tropical warmth without descending into cliché — no palm tree clip art, no Comic Sans, no garish neons. The source material is the actual color of light in Hawaii: warm sand, coral sunsets, the specific green-blue of Pacific water.

"The city is serious enough. Sometimes you just need a warm place to look at a map."

Rounded corners everywhere (16px on cards) and soft box shadows replace the hard edges of earlier iterations. The overall effect is tactile and inviting — the UI equivalent of a good beach bar menu.

02 — Palette

Sand, coral, teal, golden yellow

Four warm hues on a light sand ground. Sunset coral (#ff6b47) does the heavy lifting for interactive states. Ocean teal (#1a9e8f) provides cooling contrast. Golden yellow (#f5c842) is used for the route line on the map — warm and visible without being harsh.

03 — Map

Golden hour tiles — warmth without distortion

The lightest map filter in the series — just enough warmth to match the overall palette without destroying readability. A slight sepia tint and saturation bump push the OSM tiles toward a golden hour tone.

.leaflet-tile-pane {
  filter: sepia(0.2) saturate(1.15) brightness(1.04);
}

The ocean teal route polyline contrasts cleanly against the warm map — like a trail marker on a hiking map.
