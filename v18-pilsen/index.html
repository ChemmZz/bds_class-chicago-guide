Mexican Heritage / Pilsen

18th Street · Murals · Talavera · Día de los Muertos · Abril Fatface

Concept

The Pilsen neighborhood on 18th Street: Halsted to Western, the murals of Juan Chávez Morado to the Día de los Muertos altars at the National Museum of Mexican Art. The color theory of this iteration comes from the ofrenda — marigolds, magenta paper flowers, cobalt talavera tiles, deep teal.

Abril Fatface is a Latin American display typeface in the tradition of the 19th century poster types brought to Mexico with the printing press — dense, theatrical, commanding. Four accent colors at full saturation prevent any single dominant hue.

Design Decisions

Papel picado gradient border The header's bottom border uses border-image: linear-gradient(90deg, #e8900a, #cc2070, #006878, #1a4a9e) — simulating the cut tissue paper banners (papel picado) hung at festivals and altars. A single CSS line that carries enormous cultural resonance.
Diamond-grid body background Two overlaid 45-degree repeating-linear-gradients in marigold and magenta at 3% opacity create a subtle diamond tile pattern — talavera geometry without overwhelming the text.
Four independent accent colors Most versions have one or two accent colors. Pilsen has four at full saturation: marigold, magenta, teal, cobalt. The rule: each tag category gets a distinct cultural hue. No hue repeats.
Vivid map filter saturate(1.3) hue-rotate(-5deg) contrast(1.05) — pushes the map toward the warm, saturated quality of Pilsen mural photography.
