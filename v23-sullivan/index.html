Concept

Louis Sullivan — "the father of the skyscraper" — believed that architecture must express the life within it through organic ornament. His designs for the Guaranty Building, Carson Pirie Scott, and the Auditorium Theatre cover every surface in interlocking botanical forms: acanthus leaves, scrolling tendrils, palmette crowns, geometric interlace.

The header ornaments are Sullivan compositions in SVG: a central stem rising to a palmette crown, two scrolling tendrils with spiral terminals, two pairs of sage-green leaves, and an acanthus base. The right ornament mirrors the left — Sullivan's ornament is always symmetrical at the macro level, organic at the micro level.

Design Decisions

Sullivan ornament SVG — 14 elements Stem path → palmette shape → palmette vein → left tendril path → left scroll terminal (ring + dot) → right tendril → right scroll terminal → 4 leaves → acanthus base → stem base block → 2 bud circles. Each element references a real Sullivan motif category.
Terra cotta + sage as the two-color palette Sullivan's Chicago buildings are clad in terra cotta tile — the material that survived the fire and became the city's architectural skin. Sage green for the leaf elements echoes the botanical naturalism of his ornamental philosophy.
Bodoni Moda for display Bodoni's extreme thick-thin stroke contrast shares Sullivan's dual vocabulary: the structural grid (thick stroke) and the ornamental detail (hairline). Two things at once, always.
Deep brown header background Sullivan's buildings are often darker at ground level — the ornament emerges from shadow. The deep brown header lets the terra cotta SVG ornaments glow against the dark, as they do on the actual buildings at night.
