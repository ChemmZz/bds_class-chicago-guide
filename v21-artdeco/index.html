1893 World's Fair

Concept

The 1893 World's Columbian Exposition — Daniel Burnham's "White City" on the south lakefront. The fair introduced America to Beaux-Arts classicism, electric light, and a new idea of what a city could be. The Art Deco aesthetic that followed in the 1920s and 30s is its direct heir: geometric, gilded, unapologetically grand.

The header's flanking sunburst fans are pure geometric SVG — radiating lines and concentric arcs from a corner point, the classic Art Deco motif you'd find on elevator doors, theater marquees, and the Palmolive Building.

Design Decisions

SVG Sunburst — pure geometry 12 radiating lines from a corner point + 3 concentric arcs + 2 ghost echo arcs + a diamond at origin. No curves beyond the arcs. Every element is a straight line or a circular arc — the Art Deco vocabulary reduced to its geometric essence.
Zero border-radius throughout Art Deco applies ornament to geometric forms — never softens them. Every card, button, and popup is hard-cornered.
Cinzel Decorative at small sizes Cinzel Decorative is designed for large display use. Using it at 1rem for card names and 1.2rem for section headers creates a compressed, formal dignity — like engraved letterhead.
Navy + gold only — no third color The palette discipline of the fair's official graphics: two colors, maximum contrast, no exceptions.
