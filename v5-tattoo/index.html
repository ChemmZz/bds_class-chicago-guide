01 — Concept

Flash sheets and parlor walls

Classic American tattoo flash — the sheets of pre-drawn designs displayed in parlor windows — has a distinct visual grammar: heavy black outlines, limited color palette, bold typography, and an aged parchment ground. This iteration translates that grammar into a web interface.

"Every card is a flash sheet. Every tag is a flash category. The city is the parlor wall."

The challenge was preserving the warmth and weight of physical flash art in a digital format — keeping borders thick, colors saturated, and typography heavy without losing readability at screen density.

02 — Palette

Parchment, crimson, jet black, gold

Four colors — no more. The aged parchment ground (#f5efe0) gives warmth without sweetness. Deep crimson (#8b0000) is used for active states and structural emphasis. Gold (#c9a84c) marks the precious: tips, accents, highlights. Black is structural — the outline that holds everything together.

#f5efe0 BG
#8b0000 Crimson
#c9a84c Gold
#1a1a1a Black
#e8dcc8 Surface

The aged-paper texture is achieved entirely in CSS via a layered radial-gradient noise pattern — no image assets required.

03 — Typography

Rye, Special Elite, Courier Prime

Rye is a vintage western/tattoo slab used for display headings. Special Elite evokes the worn quality of a typewriter or vintage printing press and carries body text. Courier Prime handles monospace labels with a warm, slightly aged quality that matches the overall tone.

Together these three faces span the range from display flash heading to fine-print address detail — all within a consistent vintage register that never feels like a Halloween costume.

04 — Map

Sepia toning — the old map look

The map tile filter targets the aged cartographic aesthetic: sepia warmth, slightly desaturated colors, reduced brightness. The result looks like a well-loved paper map without requiring any custom tile server.

.leaflet-tile-pane {
  filter: sepia(0.4) saturate(0.6) brightness(0.9);
}

The crimson route polyline pops clearly against this warm, desaturated base — the same way flash outlines pop against aged parchment.
