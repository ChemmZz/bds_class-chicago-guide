University of Chicago

Neo-Gothic Academic Gravitas · Maroon · Parchment · IM Fell English

Concept

Hyde Park's limestone towers and Regenstein Library stacks inspired this iteration. The University of Chicago aesthetic is defined by scholarly severity — no sans-serif anywhere, double-serif pairing, and maroon as the sole accent color.

IM Fell English was chosen for its hand-cut, slightly imperfect character — the feel of an eighteenth-century broadside. Libre Baskerville holds body text at comfortable reading weight. Courier Prime supplies the typewriter precision of academic footnotes.

Design Decisions

Zero radii everywhere The university's Gothic architecture is all right angles, pointed arches — never curves. Every button, card, and popup is sharp-cornered.
Hard displacement shadows 3px 3px 0 shadows in deep maroon give the illusion of letterpress printing, echoing the university press tradition.
No sans-serif at all Both display and body fonts are serifs. The constraint forces a different rhythm — the page reads like a journal article, not a web app.
Sepia map filter saturate(0.55) brightness(0.93) sepia(0.5) turns OpenStreetMap tiles into an aged cartographic study, consistent with the academic reference aesthetic.

Typography

Display: IM Fell English — a digital revival of the Fell Types cut in Oxford c. 1670. Display: Libre Baskerville — scholarly, legible at small sizes. Mono: Courier Prime — academic footnote legibility.
