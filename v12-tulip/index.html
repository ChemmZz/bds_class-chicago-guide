Tulip / Botanical

Dutch Golden Age Illustration · Stem Green · Cormorant Garamond 300

Concept

Inspired by Dutch Golden Age botanical illustration — the Maria Sibylla Merian plates, Hortus Cliffortianus, the hand-colored engravings that mapped the natural world before photography. The Chicago spots become specimens.

Cormorant Garamond at weight 300 gives the display text a hairline delicacy appropriate for engraving captions. Lato at 300 keeps body text airy. The laid-paper line texture on the page background simulates watercolor rag paper.

Design Decisions

Laid-paper texture Body background uses a repeating-linear-gradient at 24px intervals to simulate the horizontal lines of fine watercolor paper — visible at low opacity without cluttering the reading surface.
Cormorant Garamond 300 italic display The lightest weights of Garamond feel like an engraver's incised line. Paired with 600 weight for headers, the contrast is wide and dramatic.
Pill-shaped tags radius: 999px on all interactive elements contrasts the rigid specimen-frame aesthetic of the cards.
Map: hand-colored engraving saturate(0.7) sepia(0.12) brightness(1.02) hue-rotate(5deg) — the map reads like a hand-tinted copperplate print.
