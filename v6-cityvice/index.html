01 — Concept

Neon, night, and the city after dark

City Vice draws from the visual vocabulary of 1980s Miami Vice — dark backgrounds, neon pastels, angular type — but grounds it in Chicago's actual night geography: the river at 2am, the green line elevated tracks, the stretch of Division Street on a Saturday.

"The whole city is a film noir set. The neon just makes it honest."

Two accent colors — hot pink and electric teal — operate as dual neon signs, each claiming its territory: pink for primary interactive elements, teal for secondary indicators and hover states. The gradient where they meet is where the action happens.

02 — Palette

Deep charcoal, hot pink, electric teal

The background is deep charcoal (#0d0d0d) — not pure black, which reads as dead. A degree of warmth keeps the dark feeling inhabited. Pink (#ff2d78) and teal (#00e5d4) are saturated to near-vibrance but not eye-burning — the same discipline real neon signage has.

03 — Map

Maximum dark tiles — the inverted city

The map tile inversion technique is pushed further here than in V1. The combination of brightness reduction, inversion, and hue rotation produces a map that reads almost as a satellite image at night — dark land, faint street grid, water as the one legible landmark.

.leaflet-tile-pane {
  filter: brightness(0.45) saturate(0.5) invert(1) hue-rotate(180deg);
}

The hot pink route polyline glows against this dark base the way a neon sign glows in fog — present, precise, and impossible to miss.
