Concept

A direct revisit of V5 (Flash Sheet) — same parchment ground, same Rye + Special Elite fonts, same sepia map. One focused change: the header becomes a proper barbershop tattoo parlour sign, with translucent American traditional roses flanking the CHICAGO title.

The reference is the La Logia sign — two crimson roses with green leaves, dark background, gold lettering. The roses are drawn entirely in inline SVG using the vocabulary of American traditional flash: bold black outlines, flat fills with layered highlights, no gradients.

Design Decisions

Inline SVG roses, no images The roses are pure SVG — 16 layered paths per flower following the traditional flash hierarchy: stem → leaves → calyx → back petals → side petals → front cup → inner highlight → tip. No raster assets, no external files, fully inline in the HTML.
opacity: 0.42 on the SVG elements At full opacity the roses would compete with the nav links and title. At 42%, they read clearly as roses while the CHICAGO title and back links remain dominant — the same balance you'd see in an embossed letterhead or a glass-etched barbershop sign.
CSS scaleX(-1) for the mirror The right rose is the identical SVG markup, mirrored with transform: scaleX(-1) in CSS. This is the simplest accurate mirror — no duplicate path adjustments, no JavaScript.
position: absolute, overflow: visible on header The roses sit inside the flex header but are absolutely positioned so they don't participate in the flex layout or displace the title. overflow: visible lets the rose tops extend slightly above the header's top edge for a natural overflow effect.
Leaf-to-leaf gradient of fills Six distinct reds from #6b0000 (back) through #cc2020 (front cup) to #f04040 (tip). The progression mimics the light catching a real rose petal — darkest at the back, lightest at the forward edge — without using CSS gradients.
