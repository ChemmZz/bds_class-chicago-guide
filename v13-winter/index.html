Chicago Field Guide — Iteration 13

Winter

Crystalline · Frosted Glass · Lake Michigan in January · DM Serif Display

Concept

January on the Magnificent Mile, wind chill at −22°F, the lake a grey sheet of ice. The design reflects the austere clarity of a Chicago winter — no warmth in the palette, no decorative flourishes. Only steel and ice and the pale sky above the Loop.

The frosted-glass route-info bar is the iteration's signature move: backdrop-filter: blur(12px) on a semi-transparent panel, giving the impression of breath on a cold window.

Design Decisions

Frosted glass route info backdrop-filter: blur(12px) + rgba(248,251,253,0.9) background. The only frosted glass element in all 19 versions.
Pale sky gradient body linear-gradient from #f0f4f8 to #e8f0f6, fixed attachment. The background shifts as you scroll — like the sky through a train window.
Restrained map filter saturate(0.3) brightness(1.08) hue-rotate(185deg) drains almost all color from the map, leaving it ghostly blue-grey — the city as seen through frosted glass.
DM pairing DM Serif Display + DM Sans — same typeface family, different classifications. The contrast between the bracketed serifs and the geometric grotesque feels like the city's contrast between old granite and glass curtain walls.
