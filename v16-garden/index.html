Garfield Park Conservatory · Fern Green · Blossom Pink · Lora Italic

Concept

The Garfield Park Conservatory is one of the largest public greenhouses in the United States, a Jens Jensen design from 1908. On a February day it's the warmest, most alive place in the city. This iteration lives inside that glass envelope.

Lora's bracketed serifs and optical corrections make it exceptionally readable at body sizes while retaining strong personality in italic display use. Jost at 300 weight is clean enough to feel like greenhouse glass — it doesn't compete with the botanical illustration quality of the typography.

Design Decisions

Dot-grid body background radial-gradient 1px dots at 20px intervals in rgba(74,124,58,0.04). The faint grid creates a botanical illustration paper feel — fine graph paper for plant taxonomy drawings.
Sidebar gradient The sidebar drifts from #f8f9f4 at top to #edf2e8 at bottom — deeper green-tinted surface below, like looking deeper into the conservatory foliage.
Blossom pink accent on labels Tip labels in var(--secondary) blossom pink — the visual pop of a flower amid the green palette.
Botanical survey map filter saturate(0.85) sepia(0.08) brightness(1.0) hue-rotate(10deg) — a slight green cast to the map tiles, like a field survey printed on botanical survey paper.
