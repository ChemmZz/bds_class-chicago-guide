Great Chicago Fire

October 8, 1871 · Ember Orange · Playfair Display Italic · Flame SVGs

Concept

October 8, 1871. The fire started in the O'Leary barn on DeKoven Street and burned for two days, destroying four square miles and leaving 100,000 homeless. Chicago rebuilt in two years. The fire is not a tragedy in Chicago's mythology — it is the origin story.

The header flames are a three-layer SVG composition: a dark back flame for depth, a bright center flame, a smaller front flame, plus a bright inner core and floating ember sparks. The layering mimics the light hierarchy of a real fire without using any CSS effects.

Design Decisions

Six-layer flame SVG Back flame (#9a2800) → main flame (#d94f0a) → front flame (#e8820a) → bright core (#f0a020) → inner highlight (#ffe060) → tip (#fff0a0). Six distinct fills, no gradients. The ember ellipse at the base + floating spark circles complete the composition.
Playfair Display italic throughout Italic Playfair at card name level is unusual — most uses are headline-only. Here it carries a sense of urgency, of a story being told in the heat of the moment.
Warm sepia map sepia(0.5) saturate(1.1) hue-rotate(-8deg) — the warmest map of any version. Chicago's street grid seen through the glow of a burning city.
opacity: 0.55 on flame SVGs Higher than V20's roses (0.42) because the flame shapes are less detailed and need a stronger presence to read clearly against the near-black header.
