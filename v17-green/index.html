Really Green

Nature Journal × Sustainability Dark Mode · Bioluminescent · Archivo 900

Concept

Two things at once: the deep ecological green of a nature journal, and the cybernetic glow of a sustainability dashboard. The dark forest background and electric green accent are simultaneously organic and synthetic.

The design rule: all tag colors stay within the green family. Architecture gets cyan-green. Nightlife gets neon lime. Jazz gets emerald. No hue leaves the green spectrum. The constraint produces a surprisingly rich palette when you explore the full green gamut.

Design Decisions

Primary text is NOT green --color-text: #f0fff4, a near-white with a green cast. Reading electric green text on a dark background at paragraph lengths causes eye fatigue within minutes. The green stays for headings and accents only.
Scanline body overlay repeating-linear-gradient at 4px intervals simulates a low-resolution monitor scanline. The effect is visible only at close inspection — a subtle texture that reinforces the bioluminescent dashboard quality.
All-green tag spectrum Six tags, six distinct greens: cyan-green, electric green, neon lime, emerald, forest green, chartreuse. The constraint required exploring corners of the green gamut most UI designers never touch.
Topographic map brightness(0.25) saturate(0.2) hue-rotate(100deg) transforms the map into a near-black topo diagram with a green hue cast — reads like a night-vision satellite image.
